What we do

We are Project Educar, a student-initiated project from Hwa Chong Institution, running in its 3rd year of Service Learning. We are an aspiring Youth-For-Causes (YFC) and National Young Changemakers (NYC) 5-man project aiming to educate, empower and inspire children from vulnerable backgrounds in Singapore, by providing them with unique opportunities that they may not have access to in their young lives. Our ultimate aim is to provide them with meaningful holistic activities that would ultimately make them more well-rounded individuals and to shape them into contributors in tomorrow's society.

More about our beneficiary

Chosen Social Service Organization

Our team is working with CARE Singapore, an SSO which helps vulnerable young people find direction, hope and success. CARE Singapore aims to employ a multi-dimensional approach that engages and empowers youths, schools, families and communities. CARE’s mission is to help youths succeed. CARE Singapore believes that every person is useful and has something positive to contribute. At-risk youths can be social assets instead of social liabilities. By being there for those who need a little more support, we can help change their future. CARE Singapore’s services includes School Social Work, Counselling, Youth Mentoring, Sports Partnerships and Teachers/Parents Training. Our team at Project Educar will be working closely with our beneficiary to raise awareness for both our cause and our beneficiary and also to raise funds.

Why did we choose this SSO?

Project Educar chose this SSO as we feel that the cause CARE Singapore is advocating for is a meaningful one which addresses a pertinent societal issue - many children from vulnerable background often do not have the means or opportunities to partake in enriching activities which can be eye-openers for the children to the world around them, as less advantaged backgrounds hinder them from receiving the equal opportunities and experiences they rightfully deserve. Moreover, there is a lack of awareness towards these children in Singapore, the struggles they face in everyday life, and how CARE Singapore contributes towards making their lives better and giving them a chance to pave the way for a brighter future for themselves. Another important issue is raising this issue to the general public and providing them with meaningful holistic activities that would ultimately make them more well-rounded individuals. Hence, we would like to work in collaboration with CARE Singapore, to not only be a part of the amazing work that they do, but also to help these youths by providing meaningful holistic education and academic education and allowing them to enjoy themselves.
